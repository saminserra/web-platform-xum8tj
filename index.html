Preston Analysis

Preston has the size and skill to play either guard spot. He ran point at Ohio, but he measures 6 without shoes with a 6 wingspan, meaning he should be able to share the floor with another point guard without sacrificing positional size. Preston main NBA skill is his passing and overall offensive creativity. He averaged 7.2 assists per game as a junior, good for sixth in the NCAA. He is lethal in the pick and roll in the half court, particularly as a distributor. He creates passing angles with deliberate, slithering ball handling and has a knack for unleashing passes with perfect timing and perfect touch. That same creativity shows itself in the open court and when he gets moving against scrambling defenses. He is unselfish in transition, frequently delivering hit-ahead passes and finding open shooters filling the lanes.
